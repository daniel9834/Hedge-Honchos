Frequently Asked Questions — School Maintenance in Melbourne

When is the best time to schedule garden maintenance at a Melbourne school?

Routine mowing and garden tidying can be done during the school day, typically early morning before students arrive to minimise disruption. More intensive tasks — such as tree work, pressure cleaning or large mulching jobs — are best scheduled during school holiday periods. We work with your administration team to build a calendar that fits around the school's operational needs.

Do your crew members hold Working With Children checks?

Working With Children (WWC) requirements for contractors working on school grounds vary depending on whether they have direct, unsupervised contact with children. We can discuss your school's specific requirements and ensure the appropriate checks are in place. All our crew members are professional tradespeople committed to safe conduct on school sites.

Can you maintain school kitchen gardens and vegetable patches?

Yes. School kitchen gardens are a wonderful learning resource and we can support them with regular weeding, soil conditioning and replanting on an agreed schedule. We use only organic-compatible inputs in kitchen garden areas to keep produce safe for students. We are happy to work with teachers or garden coordinators on planting plans that complement the school's curriculum.
