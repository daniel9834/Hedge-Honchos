Frequently Asked Questions — Strata Maintenance in Melbourne

Who is responsible for garden maintenance in a strata property in Melbourne?

In a strata scheme, the owners corporation is responsible for maintaining common property, which typically includes shared gardens, lawns and landscaped areas. Individual lot owners are responsible for their own private outdoor spaces. Many owners corporations in Melbourne contract a professional gardening company to maintain common areas on a regular schedule, funded through the owners corporation fees.

Can you liaise directly with our strata manager?

Yes — we regularly work directly with strata managers and owners corporation committees across Melbourne. We can send service reports and invoices directly to your strata manager, respond to requests raised through the strata management portal, and coordinate access with building managers. We understand the administrative needs of strata management and make the process as straightforward as possible.

How do you handle special requests from individual lot owners?

For work in common areas, we take direction from the owners corporation or strata manager — not individual lot owners. However, we can note owner preferences and feedback and communicate these through the appropriate channels. For any work in an individual owner's private lot, this would be quoted and invoiced separately from the common area contract.
