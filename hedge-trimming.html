Frequently Asked Questions — Hedge Trimming in Melbourne

How often should hedges be trimmed in Melbourne?

Most formal hedges in Melbourne need trimming two to four times per year, depending on the species and how quickly they grow. Fast-growing species like lilly pilly and photinia may need trimming every six to eight weeks in spring and summer, while slower-growing box hedges can be done once or twice annually. We assess your species and recommend a schedule.

When is the best time to trim hedges in Melbourne?

The best time depends on the species. For most formal hedges, late spring (after the flush of new growth) and late summer are ideal. Avoid heavy trimming in the lead-up to frost periods in Melbourne's cooler months. We time our cuts to promote density without exposing the plant to stress or disease.

Can you shape overgrown hedges that haven't been trimmed in years?

Yes, although very overgrown hedges sometimes need a staged approach across two or three visits to avoid removing too much foliage at once, which can stress the plant. We assess the hedge, advise on the best approach and carry out the work in a way that restores the desired shape without killing the plant.
