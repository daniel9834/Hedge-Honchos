Frequently Asked Questions — Garden Planting in Melbourne

What is the best time to plant in Melbourne?

Autumn is generally the best planting season in Melbourne — the soil is still warm but temperatures are cooling, which reduces transplant stress and gives roots time to establish before summer heat. Spring is the second best option. Summer planting is possible but requires consistent watering to prevent plants drying out during establishment.

Should I choose native or exotic plants for my Melbourne garden?

Both can work well, and the best choice depends on your garden conditions and personal preference. Native Australian plants are generally better adapted to Melbourne's dry summers and are more wildlife-friendly, but many exotic species also perform well with the right care. We assess your site and help you select a mix that suits your aesthetic and maintenance goals.

How long does it take for newly planted gardens to establish in Melbourne?

Most plants take one full growing season to establish, though this varies by species and conditions. The first summer after planting is critical — adequate watering during Melbourne's hot spells is essential. We provide a full briefing on watering needs and can set up an ongoing maintenance schedule to monitor and support your new planting through the establishment phase.
