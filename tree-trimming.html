Frequently Asked Questions — Tree Trimming in Melbourne

Do I need a council permit to trim trees in Melbourne?

It depends on your local council and the size and species of the tree. Many Melbourne councils have significant tree registers and require a permit before any major pruning of protected or significant trees. We can advise on whether your intended work is likely to require a permit and help you navigate the application process if needed.

When is the best time to prune trees in Melbourne?

For most deciduous trees, late winter just before spring growth is ideal, as the tree is dormant and you can clearly see the structure. Fruit trees are typically pruned in winter for maximum yield. Evergreen natives are best pruned in autumn or early spring. Our team advises on species-specific timing to protect tree health.

How much does tree trimming cost in Melbourne?

Cost varies significantly depending on tree size, the amount of work required, access and whether debris removal is needed. A small single-tree prune might start around $150, while larger or more complex jobs cost more. We provide a free, detailed quote after assessing your trees in person.
