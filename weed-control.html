Frequently Asked Questions — Weed Control in Melbourne

What weeds are most common in Melbourne gardens?

Melbourne gardens commonly deal with oxalis, bindii, creeping oxalis, onion weed, kikuyu grass invasion into garden beds, winter grass, and various broadleaf weeds. Each requires a different treatment strategy — some respond to selective herbicide while others need hand removal to avoid harming surrounding plants. We identify and treat each weed type appropriately.

Is herbicide safe to use near my pets and children?

We use selective, registered herbicides applied by trained operators at correct rates. Most products we use are safe for pets and children once dry, which typically takes a few hours. We always advise you on re-entry times after any herbicide application and can opt for hand-weeding only in areas of concern such as vegetable gardens or children's play areas.

How often do I need weed control visits?

For established weed problems, an initial intensive treatment is usually followed by maintenance visits every four to six weeks to catch any regrowth before it seeds. Once the weed bank in the soil is depleted over time, visit frequency can often reduce. Adding a layer of quality mulch after treatment dramatically slows regrowth between visits.
